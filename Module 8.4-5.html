Cairo, Egypt

Cairo, the capital of Egypt, is a city that beautifully blends ancient history with modern life. Known as the “City of a Thousand Minarets,” it boasts a skyline dotted with stunning mosques, reflecting its rich Islamic heritage. Cairo is also home to the world-famous Egyptian Museum, which houses countless treasures from ancient Egypt, including the magnificent artifacts of Tutankhamun. Just outside the city, the legendary Pyramids of Giza and the Sphinx stand as timeless symbols of human achievement, attracting millions of visitors each year.

Munich, Germany

Munich, the capital of Bavaria in Germany, is a city that perfectly blends history, culture, and modern life. Known worldwide for its famous Oktoberfest, Munich celebrates Bavarian traditions with lively beer gardens, folk music, and authentic local cuisine. Visitors can enjoy hearty dishes like pretzels, sausages, and traditional Bavarian pastries while experiencing the warm and friendly atmosphere of the city.

Popular Food

Fattah

Fattah is a popular Egyptian dish made of layers of crispy bread, fluffy rice, and tender, slow-cooked meat, usually beef or lamb. It’s flavored with garlic, vinegar, and tomato sauce, creating a rich and hearty meal. Often served during celebrations, Fattah is both festive and deeply satisfying.

Brezel

A Brezel, or pretzel, is a traditional German bread with a distinctive knot shape. It has a golden-brown, slightly crispy crust and a soft, chewy inside. Often sprinkled with coarse salt, Brezels are popular in Bavaria and commonly enjoyed with beer, sausages, or mustard, especially during festivals like Oktoberfest.
